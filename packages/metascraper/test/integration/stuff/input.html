Orphee Mickalad leading Palmerston North by-election

Jono Galuszka14:40, Feb 17 2021
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Whats App
  • Reddit
  • Email

A man who barely spoke English when he arrived in Palmerston North as a refugee is set to replace his former history teacher on the city’s council.

Progress results released on Wednesday afternoon had Orphee Mickalad​ leading in the Palmerston North City Council by-election.

The by-election was required after former deputy mayor Tangi Utikere​ was elected as the city’s MP in the 2020 general election.

Mickalad is leading more established names such as National’s candidate in the 2020 election, William Wood​, former NZ First MP Darroch Ball​, Andy Asquith​ – the highest polling person to miss out in the 2019 council election – and serial candidate Ross Barber​.

His exact lead was not published by the city council, and would be difficult to figure out due to the election being run under single transferrable vote rules.

In an interview with Stuff when he declared his intention to run, 30-year-old Mickalad said he moved to Palmerston North with his family from Congo in 2006 with minimal English skills.

He went to Feryberg High School, where Utikere was his history teacher, worked at the Awapuni landfill, picked fruit, drove forklifts and cleaned offices.

He went on to get a masters degree with a politics endorsement, study law and work in Parliamentary Services and as an adviser at the Ministry of Education.

He ran on a platform of preventing a brain drain from Palmerston North, boosting the city’s social housing stock and improving communication between the council and community.

Deputy electoral officer Hannah White​ said a final election result was expected on Friday.

Voter turnout was 25.06 per cent, well down on the 2019 election turnout of 37.32 per cent.
